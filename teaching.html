Interactive Game  ·  2026
ACCESS
Who gets in. Who gets left out. Why it's never one thing.

A browser-based roleplaying game for 2–6 players in which participants build characters with intersecting identities and navigate real-world scenarios — job interviews, healthcare encounters, housing applications, financial aid processes — that grant or restrict access to resources based on how those identities compound.

Designed for 200-level courses in sociology, education, and critical theory. Grounded in Kimberlé Crenshaw's intersectionality framework and Tara Yosso's Community Cultural Wealth model. Characters with more marginalized identities receive Cultural Wealth Cards — representing Aspirational, Navigational, Resistant, and Social Capital — which can shift outcomes and generate richer narrative moments than structural privilege alone would produce.

Runs entirely in the browser. No login, no install, no data collected. Designed for classroom use with groups of 40+ students.
